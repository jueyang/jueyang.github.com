The protagonist is a technologist hic et nunc (portfolio, resume, artist rooms). She once wanted to become an architect, and might have achieved that philosophically.

She has had a few loose, ongoing narratives -- the.train, very first play, poems, ongoing words -- which she believes should be harnessed into the discipline of

Playwright

I am a technologist...Yes, that's a term. It's vague, and whenever I use the word people follow up by asking, "so what does that mean?" Usually I just simplify it and say I teach journalists how to code.

God

I thought simplification bugged you. Did't you like the liberal arts way of defining things?

Playwright

Still do. The fluffy way. But nuanced discussions are not always immediately available. Many conversations are 10 seconds long and dead.

God

You think you are being labeled, so you don't bother to explain.

Playwright

Exactly.

God

You've got a reactionary approach, you know --

Playwright

Not constructive, I reailze that. My defense mechanism in a world filled with pre-judgements, impatience, and small talks is contributing to the very problem.

God

It's not an easy thing to do, but you must do it. Dismiss nothing until you have given everything.

Playwright

(beat)

You know what's funny though? When I wanted to be an architect a few years ago, I was told -- by my mentors, by the books, by the idols I had at the time -- to LIVE, and do so with tremedous humility.

(beat)

An architect is the very profession of empathy.

(finds it profound, yet funny)

Now I have empathized with computers.

God

Computers.

Playwright

Yes.

(beat)

I have become rational, efficient, pragmatic and... desensitized. The unforeseen opportunities have shaped my trajectory. I made websites, constructed information architecture, geo-located ideas and patterns. Nothing was tangible but the keyboard. Nothing was visible but the glowing screen. And somewhere in the process, I started to refrain from calling myself an artist.

God

What's wrong with calling yourself an artist?

Playwright

You make things for yourself. And that's selfish.

God

An artist can be as selfless or narcissistic as she wants to be.

(beat)

What are you really afraid of?

Playwright

(pause)

It is fear. Isn't it?

God

What are you afraid of?

Playwright

(beat)

The craft. I feel diconnected with the field, and I don't have the time to dedicate entirely given my present responsiblities.

God

Have you given everything?

Playwright

(pulls out a sketchbook from the bookshelf and flips to a page)

I had this epiphany once. I was at an architecture critique. One of my studio mates was presenting, but I didn't remember his project at all. My brain was filled with words and images and sketches... I was so deep in thought, and... all of a sudden -- beyond the control of my consciousness -- they started to zoom out, like a camera that was very close started to pull away, away, and away...until all those letters and drawings became a square. One. Single. Square. Dense with information. Yet unintelligible. The camera moved on to a different block of words and images. Close up at first, and the same thing happened -- away...away...and away!

(points to a place on the page)

I ended up with all these neatly organized squares. It was the definition of synthesis. I remember thinking, is this what abstraction is? Is this how my brain organizes chaos? Is this, actually, how the human mind works?

(more calmly)

I thought by living -- by learning things that I would not have dreamed of being capable of -- I would reach something. Another level of synthesis.

God

Have you?

Playwright

The first time I organized a bunch of code into objects I had a flashback of just that: the camera zooming out. Later I learned more vocabularies in programming: functions, patterns, modules... I have finally found the precise words to describe that moment of epiphany. My metaphors expanded: data structure, building structure; binding data to a node, associating meaning with a name. It feels as though I was learning a new language and doing stand-up in it. People laugh because the wit is real, even though it's not my mother tongue, but --

God

-- You are losing your mother tongue.

Playwright

I have been unable to address what's core to me. I live in metaphors. I love them, but they are not the core of my life.

God

Tell the truth to yourself. To the world.

Playwright

(closes the sketchbook, beat)

I am an artist.
