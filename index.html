The protagonist is a technologist hic et nunc (portfolio, resume, artist rooms). She once wanted to become an architect and might have achieved that philosophically.

She has had a few loose narratives -- the.train, very first play, poems, ongoing words -- which she believes should be harnessed into the discipline of

Playwright

I am a technologist...Yes, that's a term. You know what's funny though? I wanted to be an architect just a few years ago. I was told -- by my mentors, by the books, by the idols I had at the time -- to LIVE, and do so with tremedous humility.

(beat)

An architect is the very profession of empathy.

(finds it profound, yet funny)

Now I have empathized with computers.

God

Is that a good thing?

Playwright

I think so...

(pulls out a sketchbook from the bookshelf and flips to a page)

I had this epiphany once. I was at an architecture critique with all the guests. One of my studio mates was presenting, but I was so deep in thought with my own project. My brain was filled with words and images and sketches... but all of a sudden -- beyond the control of my consciousness -- they started to zoom out, like a camera that was very close started to pull away, away...and away...until all those letters and drawings became a square. One. Single. Square. Dense with information, yet unintelligible. The camera moved on to a different block of words and images, close up, and the same thing happened -- away...away...and away!

(points to a place on the page)

I ended up with all these neatly organized squares. It was the definition of patterns. Patterns! I remember thinking, is this what abstraction is? Is this how my brain organizes chaos? Is it, actually, how the human mind works???

(more calmly)

The first time I organized a bunch of code into objects I had a flashback of just that. Later I learned more vocabularies in programming: patterns, modules... I have finally found the precise words to describe that moment of epiphany.
God

You do seem happy about it.

Playwright

Yes. Yes, I am. It is deeply satifying to see the connections between different disciplines -- and to be able to get very good at something you thought you never would. I love metaphors. Data structure, building structure. Binding data to a node, associating meaning with a name. It's all very philosophical. It's almost as if you were learning a new language AND doing stand-up comedy in it, and people laugh because your wit is real despite your mother tongue. Of course I've endured the boring, the stressful, the demanding -- times of production. On those days it feels like working on a construction drawing of a condo that you didn't design.

(unrolls tracing paper of old drawings)

Ironic, isn't it. The parallel of the production process in all fields.

(beat)

And I've been blocked, many many times, by concepts and new knowledge that were too much to swallow. I've made a fool of myself by blowing the scope -- that's today's speak of "you fucked up the budget". I took on more than what I could chew. But I learn. I learn in the end.
God

So you have lived with humility. You have learned, and you...

(interrupted by the movements of playwright, who has begun to sweep the floor)

What are you doing?

Playwright continues, and gathers a pile of artifacts, including: a cardboard architecture model, the unrolled tracing paper, a few colorful maps, a keyboard, a sleek monitor.
God

(picks up the monitor and uses it as a mirror)

Huh. My left side is better-looking.

(finds the incredulous look on the playwright's face)

What?

Playwright

(regarding the pile)

I think I have a new pattern here.

God

Is that why you invited me?

Playwright

Yes.
