Playwright

I am a technologist...

God

A what?

Playwright

Technologist.

God

That's a thing.?

Playwright

Yes. It's vague, I know. Whenever I use the term people ask, "so what does that mean?" Usually I just simplify it and say I teach journalists how to code.

God

I thought simplification bugged you. Did't you like the liberal arts way of defining things?

Playwright

Still do. The fluffy way. But nuanced discussions are not always immediately available. Most people are looking for elevator pitches. They ask, "what do you do?", assess you for 10 seconds, and move on.

God

Why put a label on yourself in the first place?

Playwright

Convenience.

God

So you do not subject to labels, but you want a convenient way to present yourself. Hmmm.

Playwright

It's my defense mechanism in a world filled with pre-judgements, impatience, and small talks.

(beat)

You are right. It contributes to the very problem I'm battling with.

God

Don't agree so that you can escape.

Playwright

(you are good, beat)

When I wanted to be an architect a few years ago, I was told -- by my mentors, by the books, by the idols I had at the time -- to LIVE, and do so with tremedous humility. An architect is the very profession of empathy.

(finds it profound, yet funny)

So I lived and lived and now I have empathized with computers.

God

Computers.

Playwright

I have become rational, efficient, pragmatic and... desensitized.

God

Sounds like a hard-working gal. A bit old-fashioned, though.

Playwright

I made websites, constructed information architecture, geo-located ideas and patterns. I let the unforeseen opportunities shape my trajectory. I didn't want to preordain my experiences. But then... nothing was tangible but the keyboard, nothing was visible but the glowing screen. I kept going because I believed whatever knowledge I gained -- however irrelavant they seemed -- would add up to something. SOMETHING.

(realizes)

And it led to this: I have refused to call myself an artist.

God

What's wrong with calling yourself an artist?

Playwright

You make things for yourself. And that's selfish.

God

Says the person who detests simplification. Where did you get that?

Playwright

I don't know. You make things from your own ideas and you are usually protective of what you created. You don't think about the real world -- the business of living. You romanticize being broke.

God

(beat)

What are you afraid of?

Playwright

What?

God

An artist can be as selfless or narcissistic as she wants to. She can collaborate with as many or as few people, and have as much or as little social impact. She can certainly understand the, "real", world -- probably deeper than a lot of people. An artist can figure out ways to stay afloat... More than afloat. Sure, it might not be easy and might take more time than other paths. But an artist does because she must.

(beat)

Do you?

Playwright

I...

God

What are you afraid of?

Playwright

(pause)

It is fear. Isn't it?

No answer. Playwright stays silent. She walks over to the bookshelf and examines the collection.
Playwright

(as she looks for something on the bookshelf)

It's the desire to dedicate myself while I am doing something totally different. The pursuit of craft. It's far and daunting and I don't know what is the right thing to do. I don't know how to be in two realities at the same time.

God

I am going to tell you something that you've told others a dozen times: J.F.D.I.

Playwright

Ha.

God

What have you said to others when you teach?

Playwright

"Embrace uncertainty." "Do not fear failure."...

God

But here you are setting up an arbituary binary for yourself -- artist or not artist. You pine for something. You tiptoe around it. And you begrudge others the work they've created.

(beat, maybe in British accent)

Such nonsense of ill-defined righteousness must come to an end. Just -- Fucking -- Do it.

Playwright

(pulls out a sketchbook from the bookshelf and flips to a page)

I had this epiphany once. I was at an architecture critique. One of my studio mates was presenting, but I couldn't process what he was talking about at all. My brain was filled with words and images and sketches... I was deep, deep in thought, and all of a sudden -- beyond the control of my consciousness -- they started to zoom out, like a camera that was very close started to pull away. Away and away until all those letters and drawings became a square. One. Single. Square. Dense with information. Yet unintelligible. The camera moved on to a different block of words and images, close up at first, and the same thing happened -- away...away...and away!

(points to a place on the page)

I ended up with all these neatly organized squares. It was the definition of synthesis. I remember thinking, is this what abstraction is? Is this how my brain organizes chaos? Is this, actually, how the human mind works???

(more calmly)

I thought by living -- by learning things that I would not have dreamed of being capable of -- I would endure the chaos and reach that level of clarity again, with even more materials.

God

Have you?

Playwright

The first time I organized a bunch of code into objects (a programming paradigm) I had a flashback of just that: the camera zooming out. The synthesis. Later I learned more vocabularies in programming: functions, patterns, modules... I have finally found the precise words to describe that moment of epiphany. My metaphors expanded: data structure, building structure; binding data to a node, associating meaning with a name. I was able to understand both worlds. And I was able to decode and encrypt at the same time. It felt as though I was learning a new language and doing standup in it. And People laugh because I have the wit, the real deal, even though it's not my mother tongue.

God

Well, wit is a good thing --

Playwright

I love hearing people laugh.

God

-- but you are losing your mother tongue.

Playwright

(takes in the words)

It's too selfish to stay with what you know. It's too comfortable.

God

Selfish to whom? Comfortable to whom?

Playwright

I don't want to deprive myself from learning. I have earned these metaphors for my life because I have experienced the unfamliar.

God

But you keep calling them metaphors! What are metaphors good for? They allude to something that's not the thing itself. What's the thing itself of your life??

(throws playwright a pen)

What is it? Tell the truth.

A long pause. Playwright picks up the pen, hesitates, then draws on the sketchbook. One single square.
God

What. Is. It.

Playwright

I am an artist.
